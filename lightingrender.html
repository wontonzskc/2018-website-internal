I created the back drop by deleting two faces off an enlarges cube. I then used the chamfer modifier to curve out the edges. This creates a backdrop that doesn't create harsh lines.


The lighting setup I used contains two spot lights, one brighter shining from the front and a weaker one on top. I also have a area lamp shining from the back to fill in some of the harsh shadows the spot light creates. another lamp that I used was the sun lamp. The sun lamp shines at the same direction as the bright spot lamp. It creates a soft universal light.


I rendered the images at full HD. I increased the sampling size to 50 for the normal renders, and 100 for the render where the hammer is contained in the glass display case. This is to reduce the firefly created by the cycles render engine. I also unchecked the refractive/reflective caustics abd increased filter glossy to 10 to also compensate for the fireflies in cycles from having the emission mesh (the rings on the handle and the turbo).
