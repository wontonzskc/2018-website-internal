I created several cylinders of different size and stacked them together to create the shape I wanted. I then used the chamfer tool to created the sloped end of the hammer and the inside of the hammer where the turbo is supposed to go. I created a cylinder for the handle also.


The problem with Fusion 360 in modelling is that Fusion is designed for engineering purposes insdead of 3d art, which makes it lack many functions that blender has. A couple of examples are the material and texture customization features, the large amount of multipliers that blender has just to name a few. I couldn't create the turbo since I don't know any modifire/tool in Fusion that would allow me to deplicate a shape around a cylinder. I also couldn't create the handle connector for the same reason.


The good thing about Fusion is that it has a very large and realistic selection of metal materials, which is realistic when rendered. But just the material alone isn't enough to counter the downsides of using Fusion to create this model, therefore I used Blender insdead.
